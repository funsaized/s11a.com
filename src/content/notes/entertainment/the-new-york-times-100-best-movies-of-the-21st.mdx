# The New York Times: 100 Best Movies of the 21st Century

**Compiled from a poll of over 500 directors, actors, and critics worldwide**

## Top 10

**1. Parasite** (2019) — Bong Joon Ho A tale of haves and have-nots following a destitute family as it insinuates itself into a wealthy household.

**2. Mulholland Drive** (2001) — David Lynch A perverse fairy tale tracking the downward spiral of a bright-eyed young actress in Los Angeles.

**3. There Will Be Blood** (2007) — Paul Thomas Anderson A masterpiece about blood and oil, following Daniel Plainview's rise as an oil baron.

**4. In the Mood for Love** (2000) — Wong Kar-wai A rapturously beautiful romance between two married neighbors in 1960s Hong Kong.

**5. Moonlight** (2016) — Barry Jenkins A delicate drama following the life of a Black gay man through three stages.

**6. No Country for Old Men** (2007) — Coen Brothers A neo-western crime thriller featuring Javier Bardem's menacing Anton Chigurh.

**7. Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind** (2004) — Michel Gondry A wildly inventive story about a couple who erase memories of their relationship.

**8. Get Out** (2017) — Jordan Peele A thriller about modern-day slavery and the horrors of white supremacy.

**9. Spirited Away** (2002) — Hayao Miyazaki A hand-drawn fairy tale about a girl navigating a magical bathhouse.

**10. The Social Network** (2010) — David Fincher The hypnotically unflattering origin story of Mark Zuckerberg and Facebook.

## Notable Facts

- **Christopher Nolan** has the most entries with 5 films (The Dark Knight, Inception, Memento, Oppenheimer, Interstellar)
- **Spirited Away** is the highest-ranking animated film (#9)
- **Parasite** topped both the critics' poll and readers' poll
- The list was compiled in June 2025 from over 500 filmmakers, actors, and critics worldwide
- Voters included Oscar-winning directors like Pedro Almodóvar, Sofia Coppola, Barry Jenkins, and Guillermo del Toro
